Food Costs and Inflation in Malaysia

Since the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, inflation has become a pressing concern across the globe. Supply chain disruptions, rising production costs, and shifts in consumer demand have all contributed to steadily increasing prices.

In Southeast Asia, food inflation in particular has placed added pressure on households, with many countries experiencing sharp year-to-year fluctuations. Malaysia, much like its neighbours, has seen significant increases in food costs and overall living expenses, making inflation a growing concern for families nationwide.

Within Malaysia, the impact of inflation has not been uniform across all states. Differences in household spending, economic activities, and local costs of living contribute to varying levels of price pressure throughout the country.
